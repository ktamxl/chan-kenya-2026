One of the most important rhino sanctuaries in East Africa — home to both black and white rhinos, plus the last remaining northern white rhinos on earth. Rich with Big Five wildlife.

Lake Nakuru is one of the Rift Valley soda lakes, famous for its shimmering pink curtains of flamingos along the shoreline. The national park is also a sanctuary for both black and white rhinos.

📋 Safari Travel Tips

What to know before you go

🌡️
July Weather in Kenya
Nairobi: 10–22°C · Cool mornings & evenings, warm midday. Samburu/Mara: 14–26°C. Bring layers — it can be cold on early morning game drives!
🎒
Packing Essentials
Neutral-coloured clothing (no bright colours for game drives) · Sun hat · Sunglasses · binoculars · High-SPF sunscreen · Long-sleeve shirts & trousers for evenings
💊
Health
Malaria prophylaxis recommended — ask your doctor. Yellow fever vaccination required if arriving from an endemic area. Bring anti-malaria tablets, DEET repellent, and basic first aid.
💳
Money in Kenya
Kenyan Shilling (KES) — exchange at the airport or withdraw from ATM. US Dollars widely accepted in lodges. Tipping safari guides is customary — USD 10–20/day recommended.
📱
Communications
Safari camps have limited WiFi — perfect opportunity to disconnect! Purchase a local Kenyan SIM (Safaricom or Airtel) at Nairobi airport for emergencies. WhatsApp calls work over data.
⚕️
Safari Safety
Always follow your guide's instructions on game drives. Stay inside the Land Cruiser unless your guide says otherwise. Animals are wild — never approach on foot.
🦁
Photography Tips
Best light: early morning and golden hour. Bring a zoom lens (200mm+) for wildlife. Don't forget extra memory cards and batteries — the Mara is incredibly photogenic.
🕕
Safari Rhythm
Early wake-up calls (5–6 AM), early breakfast, game drive until midday, rest during the heat of the day, then late afternoon drive until dark. This is the best strategy for wildlife viewing.
